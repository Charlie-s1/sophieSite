Bad Eggs

On the neighbour’s tree branch hung the most beautiful egg, the finest glitter-coated polystyrene. Awoken with guilt, I toyed with returning the egg BUT I kept the egg for years and years, living with that pang of guilt but what an egg, what an egg!!!

In my most recent works I have been chasing the thrill of being ‘bad’. Objects that have stuck to me through my life appear together, creating narratives that test our sense of guilt and pleasure. The egg has become an important symbol in my work, taken from childhood memories of testing morality. I have been exploring childlike logic, where some items are cursed and others induce irresistible behaviour.

I’m interested in themes of identity and the parallels with metamorphosis and mischief in troll mythology. My troll figures have an over-riding sense of excitement and joy, verging on delirium, as they revel in their eggy messes.
